and Correction Insulin</h2><h3>For patients who are eating meals or are NPO</h3><article><h3>GOAL OF THERAPY</h3><ul><li>The goal of therapy is to use as little correction insulin as possible and to provide most of the insulin as regularly scheduled basal and nutritional insulin to maintain fasting CBG between 5 mmol/L and 8 mmol/L</li></ul></article><article><h3>Basal insulin</h3><p>(longer acting insulin that targets hyperglycemia caused by liver glucose production when not eating)</p><ul><li>Formulary Basal insulins: glargine (BASAGLAR, LANTUS) and NPH</li><li>Patients previously on insulin should receive scheduled basal insulin at all times regardless of nutritional status</li><li>Patients with type 1 diabetes should have basal insulin ordered at all times to avoid developing ketosis</li><li>Patients with Type 2 diabetes not previously on insulin can also be started on basal insulin if they have either:</li><ul><li>poorly controlled glucose on admission (i.e. A1C above 8.5%) OR</li><li>two or more high doses of oral agents which are being held while in hospital</li></ul><li>For patients using insulin NPH prior to admission: order insulin NPH BID (the second NPH dose may be given at bedtime instead of dinner to avoid nocturnal hypoglycemia)</li><li>Well controlled Type 2 diabetes eating meals: consider 20% dose reduction if glucose is well controlled and dietary intake reduced.</li></ul></article><article><h3>Nutritional insulin</h3><p>(shorter acting insulin that targets hyperglycemia caused by meals)</p><ul><li>Formulary Nutritional insulins: regular and lispro (humaLOG)</li><li>Regular insulin should be administered 30 minutes before a meal and lispro insulin should be administered at mealtime</li></ul></article><article><h3>Correction Scale – selection of low, medium or high insulin dose</h3><ul><li>Formulary Correction insulins: regular and lispro (humaLOG)</li><li>If blood glucose is above 8 mmol/L, correction insulin is given in addition to the scheduled nutritional insulin dose.</li><li>Add up all insulin over 24 hours from all components of pre-admission regimen and choose the appropriate dose scale according to the 24-hour insulin use. Consider starting at low dose for patients at high risk of hypoglycemia such as patients with type 1 diabetes, renal dysfunction, hypoglycemia unawareness, insulin naïve patients and the elderly.</li></ul></article><article><h3>Insulin Conversion Chart for Non-Formulary Insulins:</h3><table><thead><tr><th>Pre-Admission Insulin</th><th>Conversion (Original &rarr;
